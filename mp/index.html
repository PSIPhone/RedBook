Meeting Policy

General

We understand that meetings are important. But meeting could be expensive if there's no purpose and direction for a meeting. This page will explain the meeting process we follow at Skcript, and you will ultimately understand why it is important for you to follow these policies:

Setting up a meeting:

  • Is there any issue that you might want to solve during the meeting?
  • Does this issue really require a meeting or can be solved with emails, texts or IMs?
  • Is it necessary that the person you are inviting 'must' be in the meeting?
  • Is the agenda for the meeting sent to all the attendees?
  • Who is running the meeting? Who is the meeting leader?

Meeting Essentials:

  • Try to schedule as many meetings as possible in the morning. From 9am IST to 11am IST. You will have enough time to work on the things you discussed during these meetings.
  • Everyone should have a voice in the meeting.
  • All meetings start on-time. No slippages are allowed.
  • Any meeting that goes over 30 minutes will not be entertained.
  • Unless it is a show stopper, do not involve the entire company.
  • If you are a developer/designer, you should not be having more than two meetings a day.
  • Be respectful.

Meeting Leader Responsibilities:

  • Prepare for the meeting beforehand.
  • Be there at the meeting room beforehand.
  • Make sure you already have the required chairs and other resources necessary for the attendees.
  • During the meeting, mitigate all the other distractions from external sources.
  • You are responsible for keeping people on-track during the meeting, knowing the resolutions for problems and importantly, knowing what the next steps are.
  • Make sure all the tables and chairs are put back in place after the meeting.
  • Publish a MOM right after the meeting and share it with everyone on Allt
  • Clean up after the meeting gets over. The meeting room should be ready for the next person to occupy.
